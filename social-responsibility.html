Social Responsibility

What In The World Is E-Waste?

As you read the headline you may be wondering, what in the world is E-waste? Don’t worry, I once had to look it up too. E-waste is discarded electrical, or electronic devices designated for salvage, recycling, or disposal. The problem? Informal or careless processing of E-waste leads to major health concerns, and environmental pollution.

The Growing E-waste Epidemic And What We Can Do About It

How Bad Is It?

A lot of components in electronic scrap are hazardous if not disposed of properly. CPU’s for example contain lead, cadmium, beryllium, and mercury to name a few. E-waste workers -especially in developed countries- have reported chest pain, dizziness, excessive coughing, and loss of balance from inhaling these carcinogens. Not long after that, you can expect inflammation and difficulty breathing that can exacerbate into heart disease, and cancer. An estimated 50 million tons of E-waste is produced every year, and according to the EPA only 15-20% is responsibly recycled. The other 80-85% ends up in landfills, or are incinerated. This is devastating for the environment because the chemicals seep into our soil and water supply; and if the electronics are burned increase emissions.

Who Is Hurting The Most?

According to StEP report, E-waste is now the world’s fastest growing waste stream. The waste is being sent to continents like Africa, and Asia under false pretences. An Interpol spokesman once said, “much is falsely classified as used goods although in reality it is non-functional. It is often diverted to the black market and disguised as used goods to avoid the costs associated with legitimate recycling.” The loose legal framework in these developing countries makes the situation worse due to their inability to effectively regulate the industry. Developed countries are treating these nations like dump sites, and the environmental repercussions for man and wildlife is beginning to unravel.
